How to Know the Symptoms of Lung Cancer

People can have different symptoms for lung cancer. Some people have symptoms related to the lungs. Some people whose lung cancer has spread to other parts of the body (metastasized) have symptoms specific to that part of the body. Some people just have general symptoms of not feeling well. Most people with lung cancer don't have symptoms until the cancer is advanced.

Lung Cancer Symptoms May Include:

Coughing that gets worse or doesn't go away.
Chest pain.
Shortness of breath.
Wheezing.
Coughing up blood.
Feeling very tired all the time.
Weight loss with no known cause.

Other changes that can sometimes occur with lung cancer may include repeated bouts of pneumonia and swollen or enlarged lymph nodes (glands) inside the chest in the area between the lungs. These signs and symptoms can happen with other illnesses, too. If you have some of these signs and symptoms, talk to your doctor, who can help find the cause.

WHO Statistics On Lung Cancer

Research on Cancer shows that lung cancer remains the leading cause of cancer death in both men and women, with an estimated 1.8 million deaths (18%) in 2020. Smoking is the leading cause of lung cancer, responsible for approximately 85% of all cases. Lung cancer is often diagnosed at advanced stages when treatment options are limited According to the World Health Organization (WHO), lung cancer accounts for nearly 1 in 5 cancer-related deaths worldwide, representing approximately 18–19% of all cancer fatalities.

Symptoms of Lung Cancer

Long lasting cough Chest pain and Fatigue Shortness of breath Coughing up blood (haemoptysis) Lung infections that keep coming back Weight loss with unknown cause

Lung Cancer Preventive Measures

Major risk factor is tobacco smoking. Best way is to stop smoking Avoid secondhand smoke Stay Away from air polluted environment Protect yourself from workplace hazards like chemicals and asbestos.

Lung Cancer Treatments

Treatments for lung cancer are based on the type of cancer, how much it has spread, and the person’s medical history. Early detection of lung cancer can lead to better treatments and outcomes.

Treatments include below: Surgery, Targeted Therapy Radiotherapy, Chemotherapy and Immunotherapy
